Business Overview

  • Polycab India is India's largest cables and wires (C&W) manufacturer with ~19.5% domestic market share as of FY25, operating mainly through a distributor channel consisting of 4,300+ dealers and 205,000+ retail outlets, with exports to 85+ countries. Polycab's revenue split in FY25: C&W 84%, FMEG 7%, EPC 9%.
  • KEI Industries is India's second-largest C&W player with ~9% market share as of FY25, serving retail (52%), institutional, and export channels with a dealer network of about 2,082 across India and presence in 60+ countries. KEI specializes in EHV cables and is commissioning a new greenfield facility at Sanand with INR 20bn capex.
  • R R Kabel has ~5.2% domestic C&W market share as of FY25, is India’s largest C&W exporter with presence in 74+ countries, and has a pan-India distribution network of 4,500 dealers, 4,400 distributors, and 191,000+ retailers. RR Kabel’s revenue split in FY25 is 88% C&W and 12% FMEG.

Management

Polycab leadership includes Chairman & MD Mr. Inder T Jaisinghani (50+ years); KEI led by Chairman & MD Mr. Anil Gupta (40+ years experience); RR Kabel managed by MD Mr. Mahendrakumar Kabra with second generation family members actively involved. All companies have experienced top management and professional teams steering capex and growth strategies.

ESG FACTORS

The report references the companies' adherence to export compliance such as REACH and RoHS standards, premium product certifications, emphasis on quality and safety especially in RR Kabel's wires/products, and sustainability initiatives especially in Polycab's Project LEAP and Project Spring, including renewable energy and waste recycling initiatives.

Important Events

  • Polycab initiated a US distribution channel rejig during FY24-Q3 to FY25-Q2, impacting exports temporarily but now completed for growth resumption.
  • KEI embarked on a greenfield capacity expansion spree (~INR 20bn) at Sanand, expected operational by Sept 2025 to Jun 2026 for LT, HT, and EHV cables.
  • RR Kabel plans INR 12bn capex primarily for LV and HV cable capacity expansion under 'Project RRise,' targeting completion by Dec 2026/27.
  • Income Tax Department raids on Polycab (Dec 2023) and RR Kabel (Nov 2023) related to tax investigations; no material financial impact reported as of Jul 2025.
  • Polycab's recent acquisition of Silvan Innovation Labs (home automation) and launch of 'Project Spring' for growth beyond FY26.
  • RR Kabel acquired home electrical business of Luminous Power Technologies to strengthen FMEG segment.
  • RR Kabel rebranded to phase out the Luminous brand in FMEG products, focusing on 'RR Signature'.

Key Growth Drivers

  • Strong domestic demand fueled by real estate development, government infrastructure spending, rising energy consumption, urbanization, and expanding data centers and EV infrastructure.
  • Robust export growth driven by India’s increasing global market share, benefiting from the China+1 strategy and rising global investments in renewable energy and infrastructure.
  • Shift from unorganized to organized players leading to market share gains for larger, technologically advanced firms.
  • Aggressive capacity expansion and capex investments by leading players enabling them to meet growing demand and capture premium segments.
  • Increasing demand for premium and certified cables, especially in export markets requiring stringent safety and quality standards.
  • Growing telecom and digital infrastructure demand supporting communication cables segment expansion.
  • Electric vehicle adoption and infrastructure development driving specialized cable demand.

Key Risks

  • Housing, infrastructure, and power transmission & distribution activities below expectations impacting demand.
  • Sharp fluctuations in raw material prices (copper, aluminium) leading to margin pressure.
  • Intensifying competition from domestic unorganised sector and international players, particularly in exports.
  • Forex volatility affecting import and export cost structures, especially for KEI and RR Kabel.
  • Potential delays and ramp-up risks in capex projects and new manufacturing capacities.
  • Substitution threat from alternative electrical and cooling products potentially impacting demand.

Summary

  • India's cables and wires (C&W) industry was estimated at INR 900bn in FY25 and is forecasted to grow at a 12% CAGR to INR 1,253bn by FY28, driven by real estate, infrastructure development, renewable investments, urbanization, and data center expansion.
  • Exports play a key role, growing at 16% CAGR CY18-24, helping India increase its global export share from 0.9% to 1.3%. Indian manufacturers benefit from 'China+1' strategy and rising global demand for certified cables.
  • The Indian C&W industry is transitioning from unorganized (~24%) to organized players expanding capacity and capabilities. The industry features ~400 players with no single player above 20% market share, limiting disruption risk from new entrants like UltraTech or Adani.
  • Polycab, the largest player, is expected to grow revenue at 17% CAGR FY25-28 supported by exports and steady domestic momentum, maintaining EBITDA margins ~13% and reporting 16.5% EPS CAGR with strong RoE/RoCE (~22%/20%).
  • KEI Industries, the second-largest player with 9% market share, is projected to achieve 19% revenue CAGR and 20% EPS CAGR over FY25-28, driven by retail expansion, strong exports, and a new greenfield cable facility commissioning, maintaining 10.5-11% EBITDA margins.
  • RR Kabel, India’s largest exporter (11% export share), is set for 16% revenue CAGR and 25% EPS CAGR FY25-28, driven by capex of INR 12bn to expand cable capacity, improve revenue mix towards cables, and attain FMEG segment profitability by FY26.
  • Risks include housing and infrastructure activity slowdown, raw material price volatility, competition intensity, and forex fluctuations.
  • Polycab leads with a stronger distributor network and cash flow conversion; KEI excels in retail channel execution and EHV cable manufacturing; RR Kabel focuses on exports, capacity expansion, and product premiumisation.
  • All companies have raised their brand building and marketing spends to support growth, while executing capex plans to expand capacity and market share in the growing domestic and export C&W market.

Recommendation Rationale

  • Strong growth outlook for India’s C&W industry at 12% CAGR FY25-28, with exports growth at 16% CAGR CY18-24 and rising global market share supporting domestic players’ expansion.
  • Market leadership positions of Polycab (largest with ~19.5% share), KEI (~9%), and RR Kabel (~5.2%) provide competitive moats and scalable growth opportunities.
  • Robust capex plans: Polycab INR 60-80bn, KEI INR 20bn, RR Kabel INR 12bn to expand capacities aligned with market growth and product mix shift towards cables, improving profitability.
  • Polycab exhibits best-in-class margins (~13%), RoE (~22%), and cash conversion, justifying valuation premium.
  • KEI’s diversified channel presence and capacity ramp-up in EHV cables offer differentiated growth and margin expansion potential.
  • RR Kabel’s leadership in exports and strategy to premiumise product mix offers a unique niche growth avenue amid consolidation risks.
  • Risks such as raw material price volatility and competitive intensity are acknowledged but mitigated by operational efficiencies and pricing strategies.
