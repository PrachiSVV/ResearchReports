ðŸ§  Consensus Analysis

Overall View: All brokers acknowledge Swiggy as a leader in Indian food delivery with growing quick commerce business. They foresee solid revenue growth with improving profitability, expecting EBITDA breakeven or profitability around FY27-FY28. Valuations imply upside potential between 17% and 50%, reflecting optimism on execution and market opportunity.

Bull Case: Strong revenue CAGR (28-33%), successful margin expansion due to quick commerce scale-up, and dominant market share leading to significant upside (as high as 50% target price upside per IIFL) supported by DCF and multiple-based valuations.

Bear Case: Execution risks in scaling quick commerce, regulatory challenges including gig worker laws, competition intensification, and potential consumer slowdown could delay profitability and constrain growth, lowering upside and causing margin compression.

Main Differences:
  • Valuation methodologies differ: IIFL uses DCF, Morgan Stanley uses EV/EBITDA multiples, Elara uses SOTP.
  • Revenue CAGR forecasts vary from 15.8% (Morgan Stanley) to 33% (Elara).
  • Target prices range significantly from Rs405 to Rs535 with corresponding upside from 17% to 50%.
  • Views on quick commerce growth rates and profitability timelines vary somewhat.
  • Morgan Stanley emphasizes market share data more explicitly.
Common Risks:
  • Regulatory risks relating to gig economy, food/product compliance, and FDI policies.
  • Intense competition especially in quick commerce segment causing cash burn and margin pressure.
  • Execution risks including scaling, cost control, and maintaining market share.
  • Consumer demand and macroeconomic slowdown risks impacting order volume.
Unique Insights:
  • IIFL highlights fintech integration and customer loyalty as key retention drivers.
  • Morgan Stanley emphasizes financial strength and recent operational initiatives like 10-minute delivery.
  • Elara provides detailed segment valuation multiples and stresses importance of Instamart loss reduction.
