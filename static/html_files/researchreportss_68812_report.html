Business Overview

  • Sansera Engineering (SANSERA) is a precision engineering company specializing in forged and machined components for automotive (primarily ICE vehicles) and non-automotive segments including aerospace, defence, semiconductor equipment, off-road, and agriculture.
  • Key products include connecting rods (36% of revenue), rocker arms (16%), crankshaft assemblies (15%), and gear shifter forks (5%).
  • The company derives about 74% of its revenue from Internal Combustion Engine (ICE) automotive segments, with a diversification strategy to reduce ICE share to 60% by FY27E and increase non-auto, xEV, and tech-agnostic segments to 40%.
  • SANSERA's global presence includes exports accounting for ~32% of revenue and ~62% of order book, with key markets in Europe, North America, and Asia.
  • The company operates 17 integrated manufacturing facilities, including a key Sweden subsidiary, and plans aggressive capex of Rs 59 bn in FY25 focused on plant, machinery, and land.
  • Top five customers contributed 46% of revenue in FY25, showing moderate customer concentration risk but decreasing over time.
  • The company raised Rs 12 bn via QIP in October 2024, primarily used for debt repayment and capex.
  • Key customers include Bajaj Auto, Honda, TVS, Royal Enfield, Yamaha, and Hero in two-wheelers, as well as major domestic and global OEMs in PV and CV segments.

Management

Key management includes Chairman & MD Mr S. Sekhar Vasan (founder, 40+ years experience), Joint MD Mr F.R. Singhvi (Chartered Accountant, 40+ years experience), Group CEO Mr B.R. Preetham (engineering graduate, with the company since 1992), CFO Mr Vikas Goel (since 2019), and COO Mr Rahul Kale (since 2019).

ESG FACTORS

Sansera Engineering has implemented environmental initiatives such as the 'TrashBot' automated waste segregator with 99.7% accuracy, and supported lake restoration projects. Social initiatives include TB patient adoption programs, healthcare camps in schools, wellness campaigns, blood donation drives, and community sports sponsorships. Corporate social responsibility spans education support, hygiene improvements, skill-building, digital literacy, and conservation projects like King Cobra surveillance. The company targets increasing renewable energy usage to 60% overall and 80% at Karnataka plants, contributing to sustainability goals.

Important Events

  • October 2024: Raised Rs 12 billion via Qualified Institutional Placement (QIP) mostly used to repay Rs 7 billion debt and fund Rs 2 billion capex for land and machinery acquisitions.
  • 2017: Acquisition of Sweden-based MAPE company expanded footprint in heavy commercial vehicle segment in Europe.
  • March 2023 and FY25: Made strategic investment in MMRFIC Technology with initial Rs 200 million stake (~21-22%), increased to ~30% stake, advancing presence in radar and mm-Wave sensor technology.
  • Strong order wins and contracts from aerospace segment including direct supply to Airbus, and a growing semiconductor equipment order pipeline with a long-term LoI worth US$17 million increasing to US$30 million in 2-3 years.

Key Growth Drivers

  • Expansion and diversification into high-margin non-automotive segments like aerospace, defence, and semiconductor equipment.
  • Growing share of xEV and tech-agnostic components within the automotive vertical, currently ~15% share targeted to rise to ~20% in next three years.
  • Strong demand from Indian two-wheeler market (~44% revenue) supported by premiumization and export recovery.
  • Global diversification with increasing exports to Europe, North America, and emerging markets (~32% revenue currently), driven by supply chain shifts towards India.
  • Strategic acquisitions such as MAPE Sweden to enter heavy commercial vehicle and international markets.
  • Increased capex targeting expansion in aerospace machining infrastructure and semi-conductor clean-room capabilities.
  • Strong order backlog with Rs 18.5 billion of confirmed orders.
  • Growing market share in precision forged components with advanced product development and customer relationships.

Key Risks

  • EV adoption reducing demand for ICE components which currently form 74% of revenue.
  • Automotive segment economic slowdown impacting demand from major customers.
  • Customer concentration risk with top 5 customers contributing 46% revenue, though declining.
  • Execution risk in longer development cycles and approvals in non-automotive aerospace and semiconductor segments.
  • Foreign exchange exposure from 32% international sales and geopolitical uncertainties in key export markets.
  • Raw material price volatility although mitigated by pass-through arrangements with customers.

Summary

  • Sansera Engineering is a market leader in precision-engineered forged components primarily servicing the automotive sector with a notable presence in emerging high-margin non-auto sectors like aerospace, defence, and semiconductor equipment manufacturing.
  • The company is strategically de-risking from ICE dependence by expanding xEV, tech-agnostic, and non-auto verticals, aiming to increase their revenue share from 26% in FY25 to 40% by FY27E, while ICE automotive contribution drops from ~74% to 60%.
  • SANSERA’s ADR segment is growing rapidly with an order book of Rs 3.7+ billion and partnership with Airbus, with expected doubling of segment revenue by FY26.
  • The business enjoys global diversification with ~32% revenue from exports, primarily Europe and North America, with a strong order book exposure internationally (~62%).
  • The company has maintained stable EBITDA margins around 17% with expectations to expand to 18% by FY27 supported by favorable product mix and cost optimization.
  • Sansera has a strong balance sheet with Net Debt/Equity near zero post October 2024 QIP of Rs 12 bn used primarily to repay debt and fund capex.
  • Key risks include dependence on ICE vehicles facing disruption from EV adoption, slowdown in automotive segment, customer concentration risk with top 5 clients accounting for 46% revenue, execution risk in new non-auto segments requiring ramp-up and customer approvals, and forex risks due to 32% revenue exposure from exports.
  • Valuations are reasonable with the stock trading at 26.1x FY26E EPS of Rs 50.5 and 21.4x FY27E EPS of Rs 61.6, with a target price implying 22% upside.
  • The management team features experienced leadership with founder Mr. S. Sekhar Vasan as chairman, supported by executives with decades of experience in manufacturing and finance.
  • The stock presents an attractive long-term investment opportunity given strong revenue visibility, diversified and growing product portfolio, global diversification, strategic investments in emerging technologies, and improving margins.

Recommendation Rationale

  • Diversification strategy reduces reliance on traditional ICE automotive segments, mitigating risk from electrification disruption.
  • Strong revenue growth expected (~17% CAGR FY25-27E) driven by both automotive and expanding high-margin non-automotive segments (aerospace, defence, semiconductor).
  • Robust balance sheet post QIP with net debt nearly zero, providing financial flexibility for capex and acquisitions.
  • Strong order book (~Rs 18.5 bn) with ~62% international exposure, supporting revenue visibility and growth.
  • Reasonable valuation at 21.4x FY27E EPS and 26x FY27E EV/EBITDA with 22% upside to target price supports buy recommendation.
