Executive Summary

Indian 2W sector market cap implied sales of 0.55 bn vehicles undiscounted and 4.5 bn discounted, far exceeding realistic market size; individual company valuations show implausible volume expectations by FY2041 suggesting investor selective bias. Federal Bank sees potential NIM expansion and new revenue streams, maintaining BUY with revised fair value Rs235. Regulatory plans for mandatory ABS in 2Ws from 2026 risk stalling growth for HMCL and TVS, while benefiting Endurance Technologies by 5-9% EPS. RBI MPC frontloaded rate cuts with neutral stance, leaving room for future easing. Global clean energy investments to hit US$2.2 tn in 2025, driven by renewables and storage, yet face bottlenecks. Online diagnostics players cut marketing significantly improving margins but slowing revenue growth; positive stance on DLPL and METROHL with ~13% sales CAGR forecast. Accenture leads IT services with 7.7% revenue growth but bookings down; mixed sentiment on generative AI impact. Oil prices surged 21-25% in June amid Iran-Israel conflict, positive for upstream ONGC (BUY, FV Rs285) and OMCs margins, but negative for India. OPEC+ spare capacity remains ~6 mb/d. Sector valuations show cautious to neutral views with noted headwinds and upside potential limited by macro/micro factors.

Overall Sentiment

Neutral to Cautious

Overall Sentiment Triggers

  • Indian 2W sector valuations and volume assumptions appear disconnected from fundamentals, limiting upside.
  • Mixed signals in Indian banking sector with Federal Bank’s positive outlook but EPS revisions and sector headwinds.
  • Regulatory changes in 2W safety standards could slow down growth in key segments for HMCL and TVS.
  • Geopolitical tensions causing oil price spikes that weigh on economic growth but aid upstream profits.
  • Clean energy sector gaining momentum despite bottlenecks; investment expected at record highs globally and in India.
  • Online diagnostics industry exhibiting slow volume growth despite margin improvements.
  • Indian IT sector faces slowdown in bookings and generative AI momentum; outlook cautious.
  • Federal Bank's NIM improvement plan and frugal cost management offer selective optimism.
  • Oil, Gas & Consumable Fuels sector sees mixed valuation outlook with upstream outperformers and pressured refiners.
  • RBI’s MPC frontloading rate cuts with neutral stance keeps policy flexible but limits further easing potential.

Sector Highlights

2W sector valuations imply unrealistic sales volumes over next two decades; industry structure assumed to sustain high profitability indefinitely is questionable. Many 2W companies focused on core segments with modest volume share shifts. ABS mandate from 2026 could pressurize entry-level 2W demand and scooter momentum notably impacting Hero MotoCorp and TVS Motors, while suppliers like Endurance Technologies gain. RBI easing supports growth amid subdued private investment. Clean energy investment surge driven by renewables, EVs, and battery deployment, but grid investment lagging, raising bottleneck risks. Health diagnostics domain sees slower e-player volume growth due to cost control and pricing changes, benefiting incumbents. Indian IT services face steady demand but competitive pressures; large deal restructuring underway. Oil markets exhibit geopolitical tension driven price spikes, surplus supply with OPEC+ production rising. Margins for OMCs elevated with frozen retail prices limiting price cut risks short term. Federal Bank aims sustainable growth with better NIM, product mix, and non-interest income.

Macro Trends

Global clean energy investment reaching record US$2.2 trillion in 2025 fueled by renewable additions, EV surge and battery storage, led by China. Energy grid investments increasing but supply chain and financial health challenges persist especially in emerging markets. India targets US$1.3 trillion clean power investment by 2035 for net zero by 2070. RBI frontloaded monetary easing with neutral stance amid tepid inflation and growth focus. Oil prices elevated by Middle East geopolitics, but global oil markets well supplied with OPEC+ spare capacity ~6 mb/d. Digital diagnostics players reining in discretionary spends and discounting to improve margins, slowing down top-line growth. Indian IT industry sees structural changes aimed at reinvention and large deal focus amid mixed generative AI impacts. Regulatory tightening on 2Ws safety features could dampen lower cc 2W segment growth.

Headwinds Tailwinds

Headwinds:
  • ABS mandate on 2Ws from CY2026 could raise pricing and dampen demand for entry-level motorcycles and scooters.
  • Geopolitical tensions in Middle East elevating oil prices, negatively impacting Indian economy and oil/gas import costs.
  • Slowdown in generative AI bookings and revenue growth for leading IT services firm Accenture.
  • Reduced marketing and discounting by online diagnostics platforms impacting volume growth.
  • Potential credit costs increase for Federal Bank due to shift to higher-yield loans.
  • Challenges in PSL compliance for Small Finance Banks, particularly in small and marginal farmers segment.
  • Supply chain bottlenecks in battery storage critical minerals, notably due to global rare earth export restrictions.
  • Stagnation or slowdown risks in 2W volume and profitability assumptions due to industry oversizing projections.
Tailwinds:
  • Federal Bank’s focus on NIM expansion and new revenue streams could boost profitability and justify multiple expansion.
  • Global clean energy investments expected to surge to US$2.2 tn in 2025, supporting capital goods and renewable sectors.
  • Endurance Technologies stands to gain from ABS market expansion, potentially raising EPS by up to 9%.
  • RBI rate cuts frontloaded to stimulate growth with room for further easing based on inflation trajectory.
  • Stable to rising retail fuel prices maintaining healthy marketing margins for OMCs.
  • Online diagnostics players improving gross margins and reducing operating losses.
  • Indian IT’s financial services vertical showing resilient growth; service integration to boost large deal wins.
  • OPEC+ spare capacity and production ramp-up moderating oil market tightness, preventing prolonged price spikes.
  • Significant investments approved for Indian transmission infrastructure between 2024-2032 (US$110 bn).

Conclusion

The report presents a cautious outlook on the Indian 2W sector due to unrealistic valuation and volume assumptions, regulatory headwinds from new ABS norms, and selective investor biases, while highlighting pockets of opportunity such as Endurance Technologies benefiting from ABS demand. Banking sector maintains an attractive stance with Federal Bank exemplifying growth potential through improved NIM and income diversification. Clean energy investments are robust globally but face infrastructure bottlenecks. Online diagnostics show margin recovery but slower volume growth, favoring incumbents. Indian IT services exhibit steady growth with moderation in bookings and generative AI impact, supporting a neutral view. Oil & Gas sector reflects geopolitical risks elevating prices benefiting upstream firms like ONGC, with downstream refiners facing margin pressures and regulatory risks. Overall, sector sentiment is neutral with cautious optimism in select segments and underperforming valuations in others. Investment decisions should weigh regulatory, demand, and macroeconomic uncertainties.
