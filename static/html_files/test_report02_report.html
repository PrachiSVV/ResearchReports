Business Overview

  • Kalyan Jewellers is an Indian jewellery retailer founded in 1993 by T S Kalyanaraman, with over 45 years of retail experience and 25 years in jewellery.
  • As of FY25, the company operates 278 Kalyan stores in India across 23 states/UTs, 36 showrooms in the Middle East, 1 store in the US, and 73 Candere showrooms in India, serving a pan-India and international customer base.
  • The company transitioned from a South-focused player to a pan-India player with a hyperlocal strategy including diverse product SKUs tailored to regional preferences; 30-40% of store inventory is localized.
  • Kalyan operates under an asset-light franchisee-owned company-operated (FOCO) model, with franchise partners investing in inventory and capex, enabling faster store rollout and capital efficiency.
  • Revenue mix by geography: ~75% India (278 stores), ~12% Middle East (36 stores), and omni-channel digital platform Candere operates 73 showrooms in India.
  • The hyperlocal initiative "My Kalyan" accounts for ~15% of FY24 revenue, connecting with over 10 million potential customers annually through a network of 1,037 small stores.
  • Product offering spans wedding jewellery (>50% revenue), daily wear, and special occasions, covering different price points including gold and diamond-studded pieces.
  • Governance includes appointment of top-tier auditors (Grant Thornton), sale of non-core assets (aircraft) for debt reduction, and appointment of independent Chairman Vinod Rai.
  • Operating performance shows planned expansion adding 85-90 stores annually in India for FY26-28E, targeting 543 stores by FY28 to approach peer Tanishq's scale.
  • The company's financials demonstrate FY25-28E CAGR expectations of 25% revenue, 23% EBITDA, and 31% PAT growth, with EBITDA margin contracting slightly due to franchise margins but PBT margin expanding to ~5%.
  • Return ratios (RoE and RoIC) expected to improve to ~24% and 23% respectively by FY28 from ~16% and 14% in FY25.
  • Franchise partners and Kalyan achieve estimated ROCEs of ~15% and 32% respectively on new franchise stores.
  • Kalyan Jewellers targets to reduce non-gold metal loans (non-GML) to zero by FY28 and increase gold metal loan borrowings, lowering overall interest cost.

Management

Key management includes Founder and MD T S Kalyanaraman with over 49 years retail experience (32 in jewellery); Independent Chairman Vinod Rai (former Comptroller and Auditor General of India); CEO Sanjay Raghuraman; CFO Swaminathan V.; supported by an experienced senior leadership team.

ESG FACTORS

The company demonstrates strong governance practices including appointment of top-tier independent auditors (Grant Thornton), sale of non-core assets to reduce debt, and appointment of distinguished independent chairman Vinod Rai. Policies on purity, transparency, and product certification underscore commitment to product integrity and customer trust. Staff training and after-sales services emphasize customer well-being and long-term loyalty.

Important Events

  • Initiation of coverage by JM Financial with Buy rating and target price of INR 700 on 14 July 2025.
  • Shift from COCO to FOCO franchise expansion model initiated in Q1FY23, resulting in rapid store additions (74 net in FY25).
  • Acquisition of remaining 15% stake in Candere in FY24, making it a wholly owned subsidiary.
  • Sale of non-core assets including aircraft in FY25 to reduce debt.
  • Appointment of independent chairman Vinod Rai, former Comptroller & Auditor General of India.
  • Announcement of plan to add 80 Candere stores in FY26 aiming for PAT profitability in Candere by FY26.

Key Growth Drivers

  • Growth in organised Indian jewellery market at 20% CAGR to FY28 driven by pricing transparency, quality, urbanisation, and regulatory consolidation.
  • Transition to franchisee-owned store expansion model increasing store network by 85-90 stores per year, enabling capex and working capital efficiency.
  • Hyperlocal and omni-channel strategies aligned to diverse regional tastes, enhancing customer acquisition and retention.
  • Robust growth in studded jewellery contributing to improved margins, especially in non-South India markets.
  • Strong adoption of purchase advance schemes stabilizing revenue and increasing customer loyalty.

Key Risks

  • Adverse changes in regulations related to gold sourcing, tax, or jewellery advance purchase schemes impacting working capital and sales.
  • Gold price volatility and ineffective hedging could adversely affect margins and profitability.
  • Dependence on franchise partners creates potential operational and contractual risk if partnerships fail or disagreements occur.
  • Delays in store expansion or failure to renew leases could hamper growth and reduce market share.
  • Brand and reputation risks including negative publicity affecting customer trust and sales.
  • Intense competition from organised and unorganised players could erode market share and pressure margins.

Summary

  • The Indian jewellery market was worth INR 6.4 trillion in FY24, with 38% organised share expected to grow at 20% CAGR by FY28 versus 16% overall market growth.
  • Kalyan Jewellers is positioned well to capture market share in this growth with strong brand moats including transparency, trust, hyperlocal design strategy, and innovative customer acquisition via 'My Kalyan'.
  • The company adopted a capital-efficient franchise expansion model (FOCO), adding 74 net stores in India in FY25, targeting 85-90 yearly stores addition for FY26-28, with 543 stores expected by FY28.
  • Financials forecast Revenue/EBITDA/PAT CAGR of 25%/23%/31% for FY25-28, despite EBITDA margin slightly contracting due to franchise share, with PBT margin expanding from 4.4% in FY25 to 5.0% by FY28.
  • Return ratios are projected to rise to RoE ~24% and RoIC ~23% by FY28 due to improved operating leverage and lower capital requirements.
  • Kalyan operates 278 stores in India, 36 in Middle East, and 73 Candere omni-channel stores; Candere aims for profitability by FY26 with aggressive store addition plans.
  • Governance measures such as appointment of top auditors and sale of non-core assets bolster investor confidence.
  • Risks include regulatory changes affecting gold sourcing, dependency on franchise partners, gold price volatility, and intense competitive pressure.
  • Comparative valuation shows Kalyan trading at P/E multiples below Titan, with higher expected growth but lower margins, indicating potential re-rating opportunity.

Recommendation Rationale

  • Strong market tailwinds with increasing organised jewellery market share and large unorganised segment providing growth opportunity.
  • Asset-light franchise expansion enabling rapid store rollout and capital efficiency, supporting robust revenue and profit growth.
  • Well-established brand with core values of trust and transparency, localised product offerings, and innovative customer engagement model (My Kalyan).
  • Consistent improvement in financial metrics including revenue, EBITDA, PAT growth, and return ratios through FY28 forecast.
  • Favorable valuation with 19.4% upside to target price reflecting a 45x PE multiple below peers due to margin profile but supporting re-rating potential.
