Executive Summary

India ranks 4th in UK apparel imports with a 12% duty advantage via India-UK FTA. Indian textile exports are expected to reach US$100 billion, contributing 2.3% to GDP, projected to rise to 5%. The global apparel market is forecasted to reach US$2.37 trillion by 2030 at ~8% CAGR. Indian home textiles expected to grow at 8.9% CAGR through 2032.

Overall Sentiment

Optimistic

Overall Sentiment Triggers

  • Significant export growth potential with US$100 billion export target for India’s textile sector.
  • Market share gains in home textiles, technical textiles, and composites segments with strong CAGR projections.
  • Favorable duty structures from India-UK FTA and paused US tariffs bolster exports.
  • Shifting global trade dynamics away from China creates growth opportunities for Indian apparel exporters.

Sector Highlights

India has significant presence in technical textiles and home textiles segments with high ROCE in technical textiles. US constitutes 16% of the world retail apparel market, second to EU-27. China is losing export share to US and UK markets, benefiting India and other Asian countries. Indian composite materials market poised for 7.8% CAGR (2024-2030).

Macro Trends

Trade tensions and reciprocal tariffs lead to shifting supply chains away from China, benefiting India. Growing demand in US and UK markets with duty structures affecting sourcing decisions. Digital apparel sales constitute 21% globally, with higher online purchase propensity in Asia. India and China produce nearly half the global cotton output.

Headwinds Tailwinds

Headwinds:
  • Raw material price volatility and freight cost increases impacted home textile exports in FY23 causing a 12% y-o-y decline.
  • India's share in top US cotton apparel imports remains low, limiting potential export growth.
  • Restrictive trade measures between India and Bangladesh may disrupt supply chains temporarily raising prices.
Tailwinds:
  • India-UK FTA reducing tariff disadvantage, enhancing India's competitiveness in UK market.
  • Paused US tariffs on Indian imports position India favorably against Asian peers in apparel sourcing.
  • Projected doubling of textiles contribution to GDP by 2030, growing home textiles and composite markets.
  • Supply chain realignments supporting increased Indian exports due to geopolitical and tariff pressures.

Key Statistics

  • Textile industry contributes 2.3% to India’s GDP, projected to reach 5% by 2030.
  • Indian home textiles industry expected to grow at 8.9% CAGR through 2032 to US$23.32 billion.
  • Indian composites market projected 7.8% CAGR from 2024 to 2030.
  • Global apparel market to reach US$2.37 trillion by 2030 at ~8% CAGR.
  • India ranks 4th in UK apparel imports with 12% duty advantage vs China.

Conclusion

Indian textiles sector is well positioned to capitalize on global shifts in apparel sourcing and growing domestic capabilities. The interplay of favorable trade agreements, expanding technical textiles and composites segments, and rising export targets supports a positive outlook. Stakeholders should focus on improving market shares in high-value segments and mitigating supply chain disruptions.

Sector Specific

The Indian textile industry comprises distinct segments such as yarn/fabric manufacturers, garmenting firms, integrated players, technical textiles, home textiles, and textile sourcing. Technical textiles have high ROCE and include specialized applications such as agro textiles, construction textiles, industrial and medical textiles, transport textiles, packaging textiles, and protective apparel. The Indian composites market features textile reinforcements with resin matrices serving aerospace, automotive, construction, and defense sectors. Reinforcement (glass fiber) and matrix (polyester) dominate volume and value shares.

Other Remarks

Trade restrictions between India and Bangladesh could temporarily disrupt apparel supply chains and marginally increase prices for popular clothing items. However, these measures may advantage Indian producers by limiting indirect imports of Chinese fabric routed through Bangladesh.
