Business Overview

  • Swiggy is a leading Indian food delivery and quick commerce platform operating in over 500 cities with 50 million transacting users, 300,000 delivery partners, and 250,000 restaurant partners.
  • The company operates multiple segments including Food Delivery (FD), Instamart (quick commerce, QC), Out-of-Home consumption (restaurant reservations, events), Supply Chain & Distribution (B2B logistics via Scootsy and Lynks Logistics), and Platform Innovations.
  • Swiggy's revenue mix as of FY25 consists of 84% from Food Delivery and Supply Chain & Distribution, with Instamart contributing 14% and is expected to rise to 30% by FY28E.
  • Food Delivery segment is the largest contributor with FY24 revenue INR 112 billion growing at a CAGR of 33% expected during FY25-28E.
  • Instamart is the third largest quick commerce platform with estimated GMV CAGR of 61% during FY25-28E but remains loss-making with contribution margin turnaround targeted by Q1FY27 and EBITDA profitability expected by FY28E.
  • Swiggy’s quick commerce segment is operationally behind peers Blinkit (owned by Eternal) in terms of user base, AOV, take rates, and profitability, with ongoing expansion of dark stores (projected 2,321 stores by FY28E).
  • The company targets to achieve 5% adjusted EBITDA margin in the food delivery segment by FY28E, trailing Zomato which trades at higher multiples.
  • Swiggy’s total addressable market (TAM) in quick commerce is estimated at 152 million users in dense urban areas, with opportunity to increase penetration beyond current 20 million quick commerce users.
  • The company also has initiatives in Out-of-Home consumption (Dineout acquisition) with a 21% projected FY25-28 CAGR and aims to reach profitability by FY28E.
  • Supply chain and distribution and platform innovation segments are loss-making but expected to turn profitable by FY27E with revenue growth driven by B2B logistics and new use cases.
  • Swiggy operates a unified app ecosystem offering convenience services such as express food delivery (BOLT), grocery, restaurant reservations, entertainment ticketing, and on-demand pickup and drop-off services.
  • The company was founded in CY14 and has a strong management team including founding members and professionals with deep industry experience.

Management

Important Events

  • Swiggy IPO and subsequent share price performance declined 27% since listing, reflecting operational challenges and increased competition.
  • Acquisition of DineOut in May 2022 to enter restaurant reservations and expand Out-of-Home consumption segment.
  • Launch of BOLT quick food delivery service in FY24, scaling to 12% of food delivery orders by Q4FY25.
  • Acquisition of Lynks Logistics in FY24, expanding B2B supply chain and distribution services.
  • Launch of MaxxSaver feature in April 2025 to boost Instamart average order value.
  • Accelerated dark store expansion for Instamart with over 1,000 stores by Q4FY25, targeting profitability by Q1FY27.

Key Growth Drivers

  • Rapid expansion of food delivery and quick commerce penetration in India’s large urban markets.
  • Increasing order frequency and average order value growth in food delivery segment.
  • Expanding dark store network and product assortment in Instamart to drive GMV growth and improve economics.
  • Rising digital advertising revenue from integrated eCommerce platforms supporting monetization and take rates.
  • Growth in new hyperlocal convenience services and innovations such as quick food delivery (BOLT) and direct-to-consumer brands.

Key Risks

  • Aggressive price wars and increased marketing expenditure may delay profitability turnaround, especially in quick commerce.
  • Execution risks including ability to contain fixed costs and scale operations efficiently.
  • Possible adverse regulatory outcomes related to gig worker classification and commission structures.
  • Competitive pressure from restaurant chains promoting proprietary apps and new entrants affecting market share.
  • Dependence on evolving unit economics and consumer adoption patterns in nascent quick commerce sector.

Summary

  • Swiggy operates as a major player in India's online food delivery and quick commerce markets, with robust scale and growth potential across multiple segments.
  • Food delivery segment expects an 18.1% CAGR in Gross Order Value (GOV) during FY25-28E, aiming for close profitability at ~5% adjusted EBITDA margin by FY28E.
  • Instamart, the quick commerce arm, is expanding aggressively with projected GMV CAGR of 61.4% during FY25-28E but is currently loss-making with EBITDA losses narrowing to 3% of GMV by FY28E; contribution margin turnaround anticipated by Q1FY27.
  • Swiggy holds significant market share but trails leading competitor Zomato on some metrics such as active restaurants and monthly transacting users; however, it leads in average order value and take rates.
  • The competitive market in quick commerce has led to price wars and higher fixed costs, posing risks to profitability recovery particularly in Instamart.
  • Out-of-home consumption through restaurant bookings and events has a large growth runway, with a projected 21% CAGR and potential for profitability by FY28E.
  • Supply Chain and Distribution (B2B logistics via Scootsy and Lynks Logistics) and Platform Innovation segments are currently loss-making but expected to turn profitable by FY27E.
  • The total addressable market and user penetration for quick commerce is large and growing, with significant opportunities in dense urban pockets and non-metro regions.
  • Valuation compares Swiggy at ~27% discount on EV/EBITDA multiples to Zomato for food delivery segment, reflecting relative growth and profitability trajectories.
  • Risks include increased competition leading to price wars, execution delays, unfavorable regulatory rulings on gig economy dynamics, and competition from direct-to-consumer restaurant apps.

Recommendation Rationale

  • Significant execution headroom for Swiggy to improve growth and profitability, particularly in quick commerce segment.
  • Food delivery segment demonstrating steady growth with narrowing profitability gap versus peers supports rerating potential.
  • Instamart's ongoing operating loss reduction and growth through expanded dark store network justify mid-term growth optimism despite near-term profitability challenges.
  • Large and growing total addressable markets for food delivery and quick commerce in India provide structural growth support.
  • Valuation offers reasonable upside with a blended SOTP target price of INR 450, implying 17% upside from current levels.
