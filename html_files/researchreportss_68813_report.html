Business Overview

  • Chalet Hotels Limited operates as an owner, developer, asset manager, and operator of hotels and resorts across 7 cities in India including Mumbai Metropolitan Region (MMR), Hyderabad, Bengaluru, National Capital Region (NCR), Uttarakhand, Lonavala, and Pune.
  • The company owns 11 hotels with a total capacity of 3,387 rooms, primarily in the Luxury and Upper upscale segments.
  • ~45% of operational keys are in Mumbai, 18% in Hyderabad, 15% in Bengaluru, 9% in Pune, 5% in NCR, and 4% each in Lonavala and Rishikesh.
  • Business hotels constitute ~89% of inventory, and ~11% is luxury segment; leisure segment contributes ~13% inventory as of FY25.
  • Chalet has strong partnerships with global brands including Marriott (operating ~87% of portfolio), Accor, IHCL (Taj), and Hyatt.
  • The company also owns a commercial real estate portfolio of 2.4 million square feet spread over Mumbai (1.4 msf) and Bengaluru (1.0 msf) with an additional 0.9 msf project in Powai, Mumbai under construction (expected by Q4 FY27).
  • Chalet is developing a residential real estate project in Bengaluru with 321 flats and a commercial tower; 294 residential units sold as of FY25.
  • The company is part of K Raheja Corp Group and benefits from their real estate expertise; promoters include Mr. Ravi C. Raheja and Mr. Neel C. Raheja.
  • The company operates in high-growth metro markets with corporate-focused demand and is expanding both business and leisure segments with 1,175 keys under pipeline.
  • The balance sheet is healthy with net debt primarily offset by CRE assets; management expects strong cash flows to fund capex and growth.

Management

Key management includes Dr. Sanjay Sethi (MD & CEO) with over three decades of hospitality experience; promoters Mr. Ravi C. Raheja and Mr. Neel C. Raheja with strong backgrounds in real estate and retail; Board comprises experienced industry professionals including Mr. Hetal Gandhi (Independent Chairman), Mr. Arthur De Haast, and Ms. Radhika Piramal.

Important Events

  • FY23 entry into luxury segment with acquisition of Dukes Retreat, Khandala.
  • March 2024 acquisition of Courtyard by Marriott Aravali resort, NCR.
  • February 2025 acquisition of Westin Resort & Spa, Himalayas.
  • Q4FY25 commissioning of Cignus tower, Powai and Cignus Whitefield I & II
  • Board approval in May 2025 for fundraise of INR10bn via debt instruments for inorganic growth.
  • Recent land acquisitions in North Goa (~15 acres) for ~170 keys resort, costing INR 1,369mn.
  • Development of real estate project in Bengaluru with 321 residential flats and commercial tower nearing completion with strong pre-sales.

Key Growth Drivers

  • Demand in key metro markets expected to outpace supply, leading to higher occupancy rates and average daily rates (ARRs).
  • Expansion of commercial real estate portfolio providing steady rental income and margin enhancement.
  • Growing leisure segment with new resorts in premium destinations like Goa and Uttarakhand.
  • Increasing domestic tourism supported by government initiatives and rising discretionary spending.
  • Strong corporate and MICE demand driven by economic growth and new convention centres in key cities.

Key Risks

  • Concentration risk from high dependency on key metro markets like Mumbai (~45% of inventory) and Hyderabad (~18%).
  • Execution risk related to the expansion pipeline and commercial real estate occupancy ramp-up.
  • Exposure to cyclical nature of hospitality industry affected by macroeconomic downturns and pandemics.
  • Dependency on brand partnerships with Marriott, Accor, IHCL, and Hyatt which may impact operational flexibility.

Summary

  • Chalet Hotels Ltd is initiating coverage with a BUY recommendation based on robust growth prospects stemming from a strong footprint in key metro markets (Mumbai, Bengaluru, Hyderabad) that experience limited supply growth (~mid-single digit CAGR supply vs higher demand).
  • The firm has ~2,500 inventory keys (expected to increase to ~2,900 by FY27E) in these metros, enabling participation in robust demand growth and superior room rates due to limited supply expansion (~4-6% CAGR across key markets).
  • Chalet's commercial real estate portfolio (~2.4 mn sqft leased area spanning Mumbai and Bengaluru) offers diversification with improving occupancy projected to reach ~95% by FY27E, resulting in EBITDA margin expansion to ~92%.
  • The company has a strong expansion pipeline targeting to increase total keys from ~3,000 to ~4,600 by FY29E at ~11% CAGR, including additions in both business and leisure segments (leisure currently ~13% of inventory).
  • Leisure acquisitions include Dukes Retreat, Courtyard by Marriott Aravali Resort, Westin Resort & Spa Rishikesh, and new projects in Goa and Kerala, which are expected to enhance blended ARRs and revenue diversification.
  • Financially, Chalet expects revenue/EBITDA CAGR of +22%/+29% over FY25-27E (ex-real estate), operating margin expansion (+320bps for hospitality) and healthy cash flows to fund capex (~INR15bn over FY26-27).
  • Valuation places FY27E EV/EBITDA at 25x for hospitality, 8% cap rate for CRE, and NAV basis for real estate, supporting a total target price of Rs 1,080 per share (~21% upside).
  • Key risks include concentration risk in metro markets (managed by diversification plans), execution risk of expansion projects, and potential impact of macroeconomic factors on the hospitality industry.
  • The management team is experienced with noted executives including CEO Dr. Sanjay Sethi with three decades in hospitality and promoters with strong backgrounds in real estate and business development.
  • Overall, Chalet is positioned to capitalize on structural upcycles in Indian hospitality driven by rising discretionary spending, limited new supply in key metros, increasing corporate and MICE demand, and growth in leisure travel.
  • The company also benefits from robust demand tailwinds in commercial real estate due to IT/ITES and GCC expansion across Mumbai and Bengaluru.

Recommendation Rationale

  • Strong market position in key metro cities with limited supply growth supporting sustainable occupancy and ARR growth.
  • Diversified business model combining hospitality with commercial real estate providing stable cash flows and reduced cyclicality.
  • Robust expansion pipeline increasing inventory by over 50% by FY29E with a balanced mix of business and leisure properties.
  • Healthy balance sheet supported by CRE assets, enabling capital raising for growth opportunities and funding capex.
  • Attractive valuation based on 25x EV/EBITDA for hospitality, 8% cap rate for CRE and NAV for real estate segment, implying significant upside potential.
