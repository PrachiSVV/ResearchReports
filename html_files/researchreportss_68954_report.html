Analyst View

Recommendation: Subscribe at the cut-off price. IPO offers exposure to a leading industrial gases manufacturer with increasing margin profile, strategic expansions, and favorable valuation relative to peers.

Business Summary

Ellenbarrie Industrial Gases Ltd is an established industrial gases company in India with 50+ years legacy. It manufactures and supplies gases like oxygen, nitrogen, acetylene, argon, LPG, specialty gases to diverse industries including pharma, steel, shipbuilding, automotive, and healthcare. It also provides project engineering services for air separation and medical gas pipeline systems and supplies medical equipment.

Industry Overview

Indian Industrial Gases market is growing steadily; CY18 to CY24 market value CAGR ~6.3%, projected CY24 to CY28 CAGR ~7.5%. Industry consists of manufacturing and supply of gases for multiple sectors such as pharma, steel, automotive, chemicals, and infrastructure. Sector is capital intensive, regulated, with demand stable due to critical applications.

Key Highlights

  • One of India's oldest and leading industrial gas manufacturers with market leadership in West Bengal, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana.
  • Comprehensive product portfolio serving diverse industries with critical industrial and specialty gases.
  • Operates 9 strategically located facilities across East, South and Central India, including bulk manufacturing plants and onsite pipeline facilities.
  • Multi-modal supply via pipelines, cryogenic tankers, and cylinders, catering to onsite, bulk, and packaged customer needs.

Growth Strategies

  • Expand product portfolio focusing on specialty gases and additional end-use industries.
  • Initiate plant manufacturing operations to complement project engineering.
  • Expand manufacturing capacity in North and West India for Pan-India presence.
  • Maintain balance between Merchant and Onsite business.
  • Growth via strategic acquisitions and alliances.

Risks

  • Operational risks due to hazardous nature of industrial gases manufacturing and transportation.
  • High customer concentration - top 5 customers contributed ~34.3% of revenue FY25.
  • Regulatory risk - dependence on numerous government permits and approvals.
  • Risk of failing to win government/PSU tenders which constitute ~27.3% of revenue; risk of delayed payments from such entities.
