Executive Summary

The India CRO/CDMO sector shows strong China+1 supply chain diversification momentum, with companies like Syngene, Neuland, and Cohance converting RFQs to contracts. Biologics investments are growing, highlighted by multiple US acquisitions and greenfield projects. Major modalities include ADCs, oligonucleotides, and peptides. The sector is expected to grow over a 3-5 year horizon with multiple companies forecasting mid-to-high teens sales growth and margin expansion.

Overall Sentiment

Positive

Overall Sentiment Triggers

  • Multiple leading CRO/CDMO companies reporting low-teens to high-double-digit sales growth guidance
  • Investments in biologics with US acquisitions totaling over US$200mn
  • Strong EBITDA CAGR projections (e.g., Cohance 84% FY25-27E)
  • Multiple firms trading at premiums with Buy ratings and upside 16%-36%
  • Sector-wide theme of China+1 supply chain diversification materializing gradually

Sector Highlights

India is maintaining a strong share of API exports relative to China, supported by steady CRO/CDMO-related investments. The sector shows increased client interest across global markets and robust adoption of supply chain diversification strategies from big pharma, albeit hesitation among smaller biotechs due to funding. Investments in biologics capabilities and diversification into new modalities like ADCs, peptides, and oligonucleotides are significant sector themes.

Macro Trends

Global biotechnology funding remains volatile and subdued compared to historical averages, impacting CRO/CDMO project flow. The drive to diversify from China-based supply chains is a key macro theme influencing Indian CRO/CDMO growth. Cost differentials favor India, especially for biologics conversion costs and capex. Talent outflow and limited domestic innovation are ongoing challenges. Indian players are expanding overseas R&D footprints to access biotech hubs.

Headwinds Tailwinds

Headwinds:
  • Volatile biotech funding environment affecting project flow
  • Conservative capacity expansions by management teams
  • Talent outflow internationally increasing attrition risk
  • Limited domestic innovation and past failures
  • Regulatory compliance risks and customer concentration risk
Tailwinds:
  • China+1 supply chain diversification driving new contracts and RFPs
  • Growing investment in biologics and new modalities (ADCs, oligos, peptides)
  • Cost competitiveness in manufacturing and capex vs China and West
  • Strong client engagements with big pharma and expanding BD teams
  • Sector consolidation and large-scale facility commissioning

Conclusion

The India CRO/CDMO sector is on a positive trajectory driven by the China+1 supply chain diversification theme and increasing investments in biologics and novel modalities. Leading firms exhibit strong growth and margin improvement prospects supported by strategic capacity expansions and enhanced client engagement. Risks remain from funding volatility, talent retention, and regulatory compliance, but overall sector outlook is optimistic with select companies rated Buy with significant upside potential.

Data Sources

Goldman Sachs Global Investment Research, Company data, Datastream, Pharmacube.

Sector Specific

The sector is witnessing rapid biologics CAPEX with Indian companies acquiring and investing in US facilities (e.g., Syngene's US$37mn acquisition, Aragen's US$40mn greenfield bio facility, Zydus's US$125mn acquisition). Investment focus includes ADCs, peptides, oligonucleotides, and bioconjugation capabilities, with capacity expansions involving modular large reactors (e.g., Syngene's Stelis Unit 3 and Bayview facilities, Aurobindo's bio reactors). India remains cost competitive with the West and China for biologics conversion and capex costs. Response from China includes increased FTE deployment reflecting competitive pricing. Talent outflow and lack of innovation continue as challenges, with industry initiatives aimed at retention and hiring practices.

Other Remarks

The sector shows increasing consolidation and M&A interest, with companies developing M&A-based price targets reflecting the sector's growing strategic value. Firms are aligning to global quality and compliance standards to capture large pharma and biotech clients shifting sourcing strategies away from China.
