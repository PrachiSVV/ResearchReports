Business Overview

  • Mumbai-based real estate developer with 50+ years legacy, built over 46,000 homes delivering 20 million sq ft across micro-markets including Wadala, Ghatkopar, Mira Road, Andheri, Borivali, and Bengaluru.
  • Holds diversified portfolio of 14.3 million sq ft across completed, ongoing, and future developments.
  • Business model includes owned land bank development (11.1 million sq ft), society redevelopment, regulatory redevelopment (MHADA/SRA), JV/JDA/DM and inorganic growth projects.
  • Operates mainly in Mumbai (80%) and Bengaluru, focusing on residential (mid-income and premium), redevelopment and commercial formats.
  • Balanced mix of greenfield land ownership and asset-light JDA models, offering higher EBITDA margins of 30-35% for owned land developments.
  • Delivered 46,000+ homes with strong capital management and execution track record, including six acquisitions of projects via JDA in FY24.

Important Events

  • Initiation of coverage with Buy rating on June 19, 2025, at CMP INR 970 and TP INR 1,500.
  • Completion of first-level environmental clearances for large projects affected by NGT stay; awaiting Supreme Court ruling.
  • Launch of nine projects totaling 2.2 million sq ft with ₹6,457 crore GDV planned for FY26.
  • Acquisition of six projects under JV/JDA models in Mumbai in FY24.
  • Proactive compliance and early completion of project handovers (roads, amenities) as part of regulatory prerequisites.

Key Growth Drivers

  • Robust launch pipeline in Mumbai and Bengaluru with marquee projects totaling ₹6,457 crore GDV planned in FY26–27.
  • Strong market demand in Mumbai Metropolitan Region, contributing 30–35% of India’s residential sales value.
  • Strategic location of land bank near major infrastructure projects such as Mumbai Trans Harbour Link and Metro Lines enhancing demand and prices.
  • Shift towards end-user demand and consolidation benefiting organized developers like Ajmera.
  • Asset-light redevelopment and JV/JDA model accelerating project additions and capital efficiency.
  • High sales velocity demonstrated with 50%+ inventory sold in new launches and 70%+ sales in ongoing projects.

Key Risks

  • Extension of regulatory delays and uncertainty related to National Green Tribunal case affecting project approvals and launches.
  • Potential slowdown in demand or weakening of real estate market fundamentals in MMR impacting sales and collections.
  • Execution risks including timely approvals, construction delays, or cost overruns.
  • Dependence on regulatory clearances especially for large projects in eco-sensitive zones.
  • Competition from larger pan-India developers with broader geographic diversification.

Summary

  • Ajmera Realty & Infra India Ltd is a Mumbai-based real estate developer with a 50-year legacy, delivering over 46,000 homes and 20 million sq ft across key Mumbai micro-markets and Bengaluru.
  • The company holds a diversified 14.3 million sq ft portfolio (completed, ongoing, and future), with strong focus on owned land bank development (~11.1 million sq ft) enabling high EBITDA margins (~30–35%).
  • It has a robust launch pipeline in Mumbai and Bengaluru worth ₹6,457 crore over FY26–27, including marquee projects in Wadala, Versova, Kanjurmarg, and others.
  • Ajmera targets a 32% CAGR in Revenue, EBITDA, and PAT during FY25–27, supported by sales of ₹1,080 crore already clocked in FY25 and ₹646 crore collections at 60% efficiency.
  • The regulatory overhang due to National Green Tribunal stay affects approvals in key Mumbai zones; resolution is awaited with potential GDV unlock of ₹3,000–4,000 crore, serving as a re-rating trigger.
  • MMR real estate market fundamentals are strong, driven by end-user demand, infrastructure upgrades (MTHL, Metro lines), and consolidation benefiting organized players like Ajmera.
  • Comparatively trades at ~16.2x FY26E P/E vs larger peers, showing discount despite similar return ratios (ROE/ROCE ~12–13%), offering valuation upside.
  • The company also operates an asset-light redevelopment vertical, including MHADA/SRA projects and JV/JDA for capital-efficient expansion.
  • Investment risks include delay in NGT clearance and regulatory approvals, potential escalation in costs or slower-than-expected sales.

Recommendation Rationale

  • Strong revenue and earnings CAGR of 32% forecast over FY25–27 driven by a large launch pipeline and sales traction.
  • Healthy EBITDA margins of around 30–35% supported by owned land bank development.
  • Asset-light model through JDA and redevelopment projects reduces capital intensity and risk.
  • Strategic land bank of 11.1 million sq ft in prime Mumbai and Bengaluru micro-markets ensures long-term growth visibility.
  • Regulatory resolution of NGT case could unlock ₹3,000-4,000 crore GDV and act as catalyst.
  • Trading at discount valuation of ~16x FY26E P/E with improvement expected as projects launch and approvals finalize.
