Company Name

Sector

Non-Banking Financial Company (NBFC)

Analyst View

The IPO is reasonably valued with a strong parentage, robust growth, and diversified portfolio providing a long runway for growth. Sector tailwinds and company fundamentals support a subscribe recommendation with positive medium to long term outlook.

Business Summary

HDB Financial Services, a subsidiary of HDFC Bank, is the second largest and fastest growing customer franchise NBFC in India serving 1.9 crore customers as of March 2025. The company focuses on underserved and underbanked customers in low to middle-income households with minimal or no credit history. The gross loan book stood at Rs 1,06,880 crore as of March 2025 with a secured loan share of 73.01%. Lending segments include Enterprise loans (39.3%), Asset Finance (38.0%), and Consumer Finance (22.7%). Pan India presence with 1,771 branches in 1,162 towns across 31 states and UTs, 80% outside top 20 cities.

Industry Overview

NBFC credit growing faster than systemic credit with 13.9% CAGR FY20-FY25 compared to bank credit 11.4%; expected NBFC credit growth at 15-17% CAGR FY25-FY28 vs systemic credit 13-15%. Credit penetration in India remains low compared to other developing countries, with significant opportunity especially in rural and semi-urban areas which are under-penetrated. Retail credit expected to grow 14-16% CAGR FY25-FY28. RBI policy easing with rate cuts and CRR reductions acts as tailwind. Digitisation and fintech adoption also support access and inclusion.

Key Highlights

  • Strong AUM growth and customer base with well diversified portfolio: Gross loan book Rs. 1,06,880 crore; AUM CAGR 23.54% over 2 years.
  • Healthy net worth growth and RoE at 14.7% for FY25.
  • Strong parentage: Subsidiary of HDFC Bank, a Domestic Systemically Important Bank.
  • Access to low-cost and diversified borrowing sources; AAA stable rating by Crisil & CARE.
  • Strong network across India with 1,770 branches across 1,100 cities; majority branches outside top cities.
  • Expected healthy listing gains and medium to long term growth potential given parentage and macro tailwinds.

Growth Strategies

  • Expand product offerings and customer base particularly in MSME, LAP, and personal loans segments.
  • Leverage cross-selling opportunities within existing customer base of 19.4 million.
  • Expand branch network further tapping under-penetrated tier 3 and tier 4 cities.
  • Invest in digitalization and AI to reduce operating costs and enhance risk management and collections capabilities.

Risks

  • Potential overhang from further stake sale by parent HDFC Bank due to pending RBI draft guidelines restricting multiple group entities within a bank.
  • Weak macroeconomic environment may impact AUM growth and asset quality.
  • High attrition rates at 50.4% for FY25 (excluding KMP and SMP).

Valuation

Valued at FY25 price-to-book ratio of ~3.2x-3.4x at lower and upper price band respectively; reasonable compared to peers considering growth and returns profile.

Other Notes

Strong parent link to HDFC Bank enhances financial flexibility; company maintains prudent leverage and capitalization levels (CRAR 19.22% FY25). Average borrowing cost low at 7.9%. The company has a large and growing customer base with over 1,770 branches primarily outside major metros.
