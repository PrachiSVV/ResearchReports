Business Overview

  • SBFC Finance Ltd, founded in 2017, operates primarily in the secured MSME lending segment in India, with a pan-India presence across 16 states and 2 union territories, serving markets with low MSME credit penetration.
  • The company has a diversified loan book with ~83% in secured MSME loans and ~17% in gold loans as of March 2025.
  • It has grown its assets under management (AUM) at a 40% CAGR over the past 5 years, reaching Rs 87,470 mn as of FY25.
  • SBFC employs a fully in-house operating model for sourcing, underwriting, and collections with 205 branches and 4,294 employees as of FY25, resulting in higher control but challenges in scalability and productivity.
  • Its borrower ticket size in the MSME segment averages Rs 0.9 million, targeting loans between Rs 0.5-3 million, holding ~2% market share in this segment as of March 2024.
  • The leadership includes CEO Aseem Dhru (ex-HDFC Bank) and Executive Director Mahesh Dayani (ex-Kotak Mahindra Bank).
  • The company was capitalized through investments by Clermont Group (53% stake), SBI Mutual Fund, and other institutional investors.
  • SBFC has a cost of funds at ~9.5% and maintains a strong capital adequacy ratio with Tier-1 capital at ~36%.
  • Financial performance shows net interest income growth from Rs 7,781 mn in FY25 to an estimated Rs 14,058 mn in FY28E, with profit after tax growing from Rs 3,462 mn in FY25 to Rs 6,513 mn in FY28E.
  • The company trades at a FY27E P/B of 2.8x and P/E of 22.2x, premiums justified by its growth prospects and management quality.

Management

Key management includes CEO & MD Aseem Dhru (ex-HDFC Bank, 27 years experience), Executive Director Mahesh Dayani (ex-Kotak & HDFC Bank, 22 years), CFO Narayan Barasia (22 years), and other experienced executives with 11-25 years in financial services. Prominent shareholder Clermont Group holds 53%.

ESG FACTORS

SBFC fares well on ESG with moderate ratings on environmental initiatives such as energy and water consumption reduction and renewable energy use. Socially, it has good customer service, employee welfare policies, and community development engagement. Governance is rated good with no history of fraud, adequate independent director representation, and strong auditor relations, though independent director rotation is moderate.

Important Events

  • IPO and listing on BSE and NSE in August 2023.
  • Acquisition of Karvy Financial Services' secured loan portfolio in 2017, providing a strategic entry into MSME lending.
  • Acquisition of DHFL's loan book via pass-through certificate investments in 2019 to secure funding access and build collection infrastructure.
  • November 2024 registration as a corporate agent for insurance products, expanding fee income streams.
  • Q2FY25 tightening of credit filters and slowdown in MSME disbursements responding to early signs of asset quality stress.

Key Growth Drivers

  • Large and underpenetrated MSME lending market with ~Rs3.2 trillion opportunity in Rs0.5-3 mn ticket size segment.
  • Pan-India presence across multiple states with low MSME credit penetration providing geographic diversification and growth runway.
  • Focus on secured MSME loans and gold loans which provide risk diversification and asset liability matching benefits.
  • In-house operating model for sourcing, underwriting, and collections leading to better asset quality and customer loyalty.
  • Management’s experience and proactive cycle management fostering prudent growth and credit quality.

Key Risks

  • Scalability beyond Rs250 billion AUM is untested due to high employee attrition and low productivity.
  • Lack of Housing Finance Company license limiting access to lower cost NHB refinancing and benefits of SARFAESI.
  • Potential disruption in MSME business models from formal sector competition posing asset quality and growth challenges.

Summary

  • SBFC Finance Ltd has built a pan-India secured MSME lending franchise with a strong in-house operating model supporting robust asset quality and steady profitability improvement.
  • The company’s AUM has grown at a 40% CAGR in the last five years, with FY25 AUM at Rs 87,470 mn and an expected 27% CAGR over the next three years.
  • SBFC focuses on secured MSME loans (ticket size Rs0.5-3mn) and gold loans (~15-20% of AUM) for diversification and ALM benefits.
  • The management team is experienced with deep industry knowledge, helping the company navigate credit cycles prudently, defocusing on unsecured loans ahead of credit stress.
  • Operating leverage and financial leverage are expected to drive RoE expansion from ~12.7% in FY25 to 15%+ by FY28.
  • SBFC maintains a strong capital base with a Tier-1 ratio of ~36% and a diversified liability profile including co-lending arrangements, which supports liquidity and funding cost management.
  • While asset quality is stable, challenges include low employee productivity and high attrition, which could constrain scaling beyond Rs200-250bn AUM.
  • The company trades at premium valuation (FY27E P/E 22.2x and P/B 2.8x) justified by growth prospects, management quality and conservative underwriting with expected 15%-18% IRR over five years.
  • Key risks to the investment thesis include scalability constraints, absence of a housing finance license limiting low-cost funding, and potential disruption in the MSME sector affecting growth and asset quality.

Recommendation Rationale

  • Strong historical growth with 40% AUM CAGR over 5 years and expected 27% CAGR ahead.
  • Experienced management team with proven credit cycle navigation abilities.
  • Pan-India distribution and focus on secured MSME lending, a large and growing market segment with ~Rs3.2tn market size for Rs0.5-3mn tickets.
  • Prudent liability management with diversified funding sources and strong capital adequacy ratios.
  • Favourable valuation based on two-stage excess return on equity model with 15%-18% IRR potential.
