  • Earnings growth revival expected from 2HFY26 onward (16.1% CAGR for banks in FY27E)

Banks show divergent performance: private banks mixed, PSU banks underperforming. Credit growth expected 11.5% in FY26E with recovery to 13% in FY27E. NBFC vehicle financiers poised for margin expansion (NIM up 10-25bp) and growth from 2HFY26. Insurance sector faces regulatory uncertainty; life insurance sees stable VNB margins. Capital markets impacted by regulatory changes but fundamentals remain healthy. Wealth management and AMCs offer growth opportunities.

Macro Trends

Declining interest rates with cumulative 100bp repo cut by RBI and CRR reduction affect lending yields and NIMs. Economic growth outlook modest. Regulatory interventions affecting F&O segments and health insurance pricing. Credit cost trends improving in unsecured retail and MFI segments from 2HFY26. Market sentiments fluctuate affecting capital markets and non-lending financials.

Headwinds Tailwinds

Headwinds:
  • Near-term earnings softness due to repo rate cuts impacting lending yields and NIM compression
  • Slower unsecured retail lending amid stressed asset quality and tighter underwriting
  • Regulatory changes impacting capital markets and insurance sectors
  • Modest loan growth in PSU banks and margin pressures
  • Stress in MFI and unsecured loan segments for SFBs
Tailwinds:
  • CRR cut from Sepâ€™25 aiding funding cost reduction
  • Improvement in early-stage delinquencies from 2HFY26
  • NBFC vehicle financiers benefiting from rate cuts with NIM expansion of 10-25bp
  • Strong market momentum in capital markets after tariff fears wane
  • Growing wealth management inflows and steady VNB margins in life insurance
  • Selective earnings growth recovery in mid-sized and MFI-focused banks

Key Statistics

  • Bank Nifty and Nifty Financials posted 8.1% and 14.6% returns respectively over past 12 months, outperforming Nifty-50's 5.4%.
  • NBFC vehicle financiers expected NIM expansion of 10-25 basis points post rate cuts.
  • Credit growth slowed to 8.9% YoY in May'25, forecasted at 11.5% in FY26 and gradual recovery to 13% in FY27.
  • Earnings growth for Private Banks to bottom at ~9.7% in FY26, recovering to ~16.1% in FY27.
  • PSU banks earnings growth expected to moderate to 7% CAGR over FY25-27 vs 38% CAGR during FY22-25.
  • Life insurance industry showed 9% growth in FY25; private insurers' APE rose 12% YoY, LIC's declined 16%.
  • Selected BFSI stocks delivered outstanding 12-month returns: BSE (187%), HDFC Life (28%), PNB Housing Finance (24%), Nuvama (33%).

Conclusion

MOFSL maintains a cautiously optimistic outlook for BFSI, expecting earnings recovery from 2HFY26 fueled by rate cuts and improving asset quality. Preference for private banks over PSU banks, selective NBFCs (especially vehicle financiers), and non-lending financials is emphasized. Valuations appear reasonable to support selective BUYs on 16 top picks amidst macro uncertainties and regulatory changes.
