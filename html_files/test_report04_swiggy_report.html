Business Overview

  • Swiggy is India’s second-largest food tech company offering food delivery (FD), quick commerce (QC), and other verticals through a unified app.
  • Swiggy operates in Food delivery (49.4% revenue share in FY24), Supply chain and distribution (38.8%), Quick commerce (8.8%), Out-of-home consumption (1.3%), and Platform innovations (0.9%).
  • Swiggy had 14.7 million monthly transacting users (MTUs) and approximately 252k restaurant partners for FD as of 4QFY25.
  • Swiggy’s Quick commerce (Instamart) operates ~1,021 dark stores (FY25) and reported 9.8 million MTUs in 4QFY25.
  • Swiggy’s FY25 reported revenue was Rs152.3 billion, with an EBITDA margin of -18.3%.
  • The company aims to deliver 28% revenue CAGR over FY25-28ii, becoming EBITDA positive by FY27ii and PAT positive by FY28ii.
  • Swiggy’s Food delivery business had a Gross Order Value (GOV) of USD 3.4 bn annualized in 4QFY25, with Swiggy at 43% market share versus Zomato.
  • QC market size is expected to reach USD40bn by FY30ii growing over 50% CAGR, where Swiggy is among the top 3 players with a GOV of USD2.7bn in 1QFY26.
  • Swiggy has diversified revenue streams including commissions, advertising, platform fees, and delivery charges across its verticals.

Management

Key management includes Sriharsha Majety (MD and CEO) with 10+ years experience at Swiggy; Nandan Reddy (WTD - Head of Innovation); Rahul Bothra (CFO) with extensive finance experience; Amitesh Jha (CEO – Instamart). The Board features Anand Kripalu (Chairman), Shailesh Haribhakti, Suparna Mitra as Independent Directors, and nominee directors from key investors like Prosus and Accel.

Important Events

  • Swiggy IPO launched in Nov 2024 at approx. USD 11.3bn valuation.
  • Acquisitions: Lynks Logistics (Aug 2023), Dineout (Jul 2022), Rapido minority stake (Apr 2022), Scootsy and Supr Daily in 2018, among others.
  • Launched Swiggy-HDFC co-branded credit card (2023).
  • Launched Swiggy One BLCK premium membership (Dec 2024).
  • Executed rapid expansion of Quick Commerce dark stores from 523 (4QFY24) to 1021 (4QFY25).
  • Management changes: Several leadership departures and new appointments during 2023-2024.

Key Growth Drivers

  • Structural growth in Indian online food delivery with 18-22% GOV growth medium term.
  • Rapidly expanding quick commerce market growing over 50% CAGR and expected to reach USD40bn by FY30ii.
  • Unified app providing superior cross-selling and engagement leading to higher average spends.
  • Aggressive expansion and densification of dark store network to increase Quick Commerce scale and profitability.
  • Loyalty programs driving higher frequency and monetization per user.
  • Innovation initiatives such as 10-minute Bolt delivery supporting new use cases and customer acquisition.
  • Large underpenetrated out-of-home consumption market for digital disruption.
  • Rising digital adoption and urban penetration expansion.
  • Strong advertising and platform enablement revenues increasing overall profitability.

Key Risks

  • Potential disruption in food delivery market by new entrants or ONDC players.
  • Intense competition in Quick Commerce leading to prolonged cash burn and compressed margins.
  • Broad-based slowdown in consumer sentiment could dampen Gross Order Value and revenues.
  • Concentration in urban markets; inability to expand beyond metros and Tier 1 cities could constrain growth.
  • Regulatory risks including gig worker laws, food/product quality compliance, and FDI policies.
  • Challenges to rider ecosystem stability and costs due to emerging labor laws and regulations.
  • Failure or delay in scaling new initiatives like Dineout or innovations.
  • Execution risk across micro-markets could lead to loss of market share and profitability impact.

Summary

  • Swiggy is an established online food tech platform with leadership in Food Delivery and Quick Commerce segments in India.
  • FY25A reported consolidated revenue stood at Rs152.3 billion, growing 35.4% YoY, driven by robust growth in both FD and QC segments.
  • Food Delivery segment accounts for approximately 49.4% of revenues with 14.7 mn MTUs in FY25, projected to grow at 18% CAGR over FY25-28ii with improving EBITDA margin forecast to reach 7% by FY28ii.
  • Quick Commerce segment (Instamart) grew rapidly with annualized GOV of USD2.7bn in early FY26 and plans near 50% CAGR to FY28ii, but remains EBITDA negative, breakeven expected by FY29ii.
  • Swiggy’s business model centers on a unified app strategy increasing cross-selling of services and engaging 35% users multi-service usage in 4QFY25 compared to 15% in FY22.
  • They have successful loyalty schemes: Swiggy One, One Lite, and OneBLCK; and a co-branded credit card with HDFC Bank, providing customer retention and premium benefits.
  • Competition is intense, with Swiggy trailing Eternal (Zomato) by 7-8 quarters in FD and QC GOV and profitability, though it has begun arresting market share decline recently.
  • Rapid dark store expansion (doubling in FY25) and improving Average Order Value (AOV) in QC underpin growth but contribute to current cash burn and negative EBITDA margins.
  • The report details risks including potential market disruption, fierce QC competition, consumer sentiment slowdown, regulatory risks, rider ecosystem challenges, and execution risks.
  • Swiggy has a strong management team led by CEO Sriharsha Majety with a skilled leadership team.
  • Valuation at Rs535 implies 4.1x FY26ii EV/Sales and 3.3x FY27ii EV/Sales, well below Eternal’s multiples, reflecting execution lag and growth potential.
  • Scenario analysis shows bear case 36% downside vs bull case 2x upside from current price, indicating favorable risk/reward.
  • Swiggy’s market cap of USD10.3bn undervalues QC and other business units, currently implied at only USD1.8bn.
  • Swiggy has made strategic acquisitions including Lynks Logistics and Dineout to expand service offerings beyond core food delivery.

Recommendation Rationale

  • Strong revenue growth outlook with 28% CAGR over FY25-28ii driven by Food Delivery and Quick Commerce expansion.
  • Dominant duopoly in Indian Food Delivery with increasing market share stabilization recently.
  • High potential from Quick Commerce, expected to grow at 54% CAGR despite current losses, with breakeven expected by FY29ii.
  • Valuation discount vs. Eternal provides asymmetric upside with potential 50% implicit price appreciation.
  • Unified app strategy improves customer acquisition, cross-selling, and monetization across verticals.
  • Loyalty programs and fintech integrations (credit card) enhance customer retention and engagement.
  • Experienced management team with clear strategic plans and execution focus.
  • Risks like prolonged competition, regulatory changes, and execution challenges are noted but do not outweigh growth potential.
