Business Overview

  • Swiggy Ltd operates in the food delivery and quick commerce (Q-commerce) sectors primarily in India, with a focus on a duopoly market alongside Eternal (Zomato).
  • The company expects its food delivery business to grow at a 15.8% CAGR between FY25 and FY28, maintaining a relative market share around 43%.
  • Swiggy is aggressively investing in expanding its Q-commerce infrastructure, now managing approximately 4 million sq.ft. of dark store space, targeting a market share of about 22% by FY31 in a Quick Commerce TAM projected to reach US$57 billion by 2030.
  • Swiggy's food delivery business is projected to deliver adjusted EBITDA margins of 4.8% by FY28, versus Eternal's 6%.
  • Quick Commerce steady-state margins are forecasted at 2.6% by FY31 for Swiggy (Instamart), lower than Eternal's Blinkit at 4.7%.
  • The company's consolidated EBITDA breakeven is expected by FY28, factoring in significant losses projected in Quick Commerce (over US$1.2 billion).
  • The company serves approximately 19.3 million monthly transacting customers in food delivery and 17.1 million in Quick Commerce by FY28, with average order values increasing to Rs 492 for food delivery.
  • Swiggy launched 'Bolt', a 10-minute food delivery service scaled to 500+ cities, contributing 12% of food delivery orders as of May 2025.
  • Swiggy has been strengthening management with multiple senior hires for Swiggy Instamart and other divisions.
  • Swiggy's IPO price was Rs 390/share in November 2024; stock price at report time (June 2, 2025) is Rs 333.30, trading at a 48%-52% discount in valuation multiples versus Eternal (Zomato).

Management

Swiggy has strengthened its management team with several senior hires for the Quick Commerce division (Instamart) and other units, including senior vice presidents and vice presidents in operations, marketing, growth, and product areas.

ESG FACTORS

Report mentions regulatory risks including gig worker welfare regulations in Indian states (Rajasthan, Telangana, Karnataka) and potential impact from changes in retail FDI or labor laws.

Important Events

  • Swiggy launched 'Bolt' 10-minute food delivery service scaled to 500+ cities, contributing 12% of orders as of May 2025.
  • Completed IPO in November 2024 at Rs 390/share; stock price declined ~21% since listing.
  • Aggressive expansion of Quick Commerce dark store network with more than doubling of space to ~4 million sq.ft.
  • Multiple senior management hires in Swiggy Instamart and related divisions during 2024-2025 to strengthen leadership team.

Key Growth Drivers

  • Expanding Quick Commerce total addressable market (TAM), up to US$57bn by 2030 from US$42bn prior estimate.
  • Growing monthly transacting customers in both food delivery and Quick Commerce segments.
  • Improving average order values and gross order values in both businesses.
  • Rapid expansion of dark store infrastructure, especially outside metro cities for Quick Commerce.
  • Increased online grocery penetration facilitated by Quick Commerce.
  • Improved execution and product innovation such as 10-minute delivery via 'Bolt'.
  • Steady market share maintenance and slight gains in food delivery segment against primary competitor Eternal.

Key Risks

  • Weaker execution leading to loss of market share in both Quick Commerce and food delivery segments.
  • Higher than expected investments and cash burn in Quick Commerce due to intense competition.
  • Regulatory risks related to gig worker welfare regulations in Indian states and foreign direct investment (FDI) policy changes in retail.
  • Food delivery industry maturing, causing slower growth and potential for market saturation.
  • Potential dilution risk due to further fundraising if cash burn intensifies.
  • Liquidity events from pre-IPO investor sell-offs creating stock price pressure.

Summary

  • Swiggy Ltd operates mainly in India's food delivery and Quick Commerce markets, facing competition primarily from Eternal Ltd. (Zomato/Blinkit).
  • The company is expected to sustain its food delivery gross order value (GOV) growth at ~15.8% CAGR over FY25-28 and Quick Commerce GOV at 63% CAGR over the same period.
  • Food delivery adjusted EBITDA margins are forecasted to improve to 4.8% by FY28, narrowing the gap versus Eternal which targets 6%.
  • Quick Commerce steady-state adjusted EBITDA margins for Swiggy are expected to be 2.6% by FY31, lower than Eternal's Blinkit at 4.7%, reflecting differences in throughput and cost structure.
  • Swiggy's consolidated EBITDA breakeven is anticipated around FY28 after cumulative Quick Commerce losses of over US$1.2 billion.
  • Swiggy's Quick Commerce market share is projected at 22% by FY31 with relative market share vs Eternal improving slightly from 33% to 37% by FY28.
  • The company is expanding its Quick Commerce network aggressively, doubling dark store space to about 4 million square feet and increasing presence in tier 2-3 cities.
  • Key risks include market share loss in food delivery and Quick Commerce due to execution failures, higher-than-expected competitive investment, and regulatory risks related to gig workers and retail FDI.
  • Swiggy's valuation is at a 48%-52% discount to Eternal based on EV/adjusted revenue and EV/GOV multiples reflecting execution lag and market share difference.
  • The report incorporates three risk-reward scenarios - base, bull, and bear cases with respective target prices of Rs 405, Rs 654, and Rs 202 and assigns weights 60%, 20%, and 20%.
  • Recent management strengthening and initiatives like a 10-minute food delivery service 'Bolt' highlight focus on improving execution and market share.
  • Market participants are underestimating Swiggy's Quick Commerce value, with potential upside of about 22% from current stock prices based on fair value assessment.
  • Swiggy's financials show progressive revenue growth (Rs 152bn in FY25E to Rs 370bn in FY28E) and reducing losses towards profitability with adjusted EBITDA turning positive in FY28E.

Recommendation Rationale

  • Swiggy controls a significant share in both food delivery (~43%) and expanding Quick Commerce (~22%) in India with promising growth prospects.
  • Projected food delivery business margin improvements and Quick Commerce expansion position Swiggy well for medium-term profitability improvements.
  • Valuation discounts to market leader Eternal reflect risk but also imply upside potential as Swiggy narrows margin and market share gaps.
  • Strong balance sheet and positive cash flow from food delivery business support ongoing aggressive investments in Quick Commerce, enabling market share maintenance and expansion.
  • Recent initiatives, such as the 10-minute delivery service and management hires, indicate improving execution capabilities.
  • Sector trends favor Quick Commerce market expansion to US$57 billion by 2030, of which Swiggy is well-positioned to benefit.
  • Key risks are mitigated by Swiggy's current market position and financial resources, supporting confidence in reaching EBITDA breakeven by FY28.
