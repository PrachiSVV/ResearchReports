Business Overview

  • Siemens Energy India Ltd (SEL) is a power pureplay company with exclusive rights for Siemens Energy products and solutions in South Asia (India, Bhutan, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Maldives), excluding Wind Power and Compressors.
  • SEL offers a broad portfolio across decarbonization, power generation, power evacuation, grid automation, EPC services, green hydrogen, battery storage, and associated digital and O&M solutions.
  • The company serves power utilities, independent power producers, EPC firms, industrial customers, data centers, infrastructure, and defense sectors.
  • SEL reported a 22.6% EBITDA margin in H1FY25 and has an order backlog of INR 150 billion (2.1x FY25E revenue), providing strong growth visibility.
  • SEL’s products include gas and steam turbines (10 kW to 250 MW), large generators, Air and Gas Insulated Switchgear (up to 800 kV AIS and 420 kV GIS), transformers, reactors, traction transformers, instrument transformers, HVDC VSC technology, FACTS solutions, and EPC services.
  • SEL's services cover lifecycle asset management, modernization, digitalization, and consulting.
  • The company benefits structurally from India’s decarbonization drive, power transmission and distribution (T&D) investments, with a base T&D equipment addressable market growing 2.5x post-COVID to INR 600-700 billion annually including exports.
  • SEL is positioned among global technology leaders in high-voltage transmission alongside Hitachi Energy and GE Vernova.

Key Growth Drivers

  • India’s strong decarbonization and clean energy push expanding market for green hydrogen, battery storage, and hydrogen-blend gas turbines.
  • Rising investments in India’s power transmission infrastructure, including HVDC and FACTS technologies needed for renewable integration.
  • Growing regional exclusivity rights in South Asia allowing market expansion and exports.
  • Expansion in industrial steam turbine and waste heat recovery solutions supporting industrial decarbonization.
  • Digitalization and automation solutions creating higher value add and recurring service revenues.
  • Large Indian market demand growth post-COVID resulting in 2.5x increase in T&D base equipment market size.

Key Risks

  • Delayed adoption or ramp-up of new technologies such as PEM electrolyzers and hydrogen turbines.
  • Increased competition from global and local players in transmission and generation segments.
  • Execution risks related to EPC projects and order backlog conversion.
  • Regulatory or policy shifts in India affecting power sector investments.
  • Macroeconomic factors impacting capital expenditure by utilities and industrial customers.

Summary

  • Siemens Energy India Ltd is positioned as a leader in the Indian power sector focusing on decarbonization, power transmission, and generation technologies with exclusive regional rights in South Asia.
  • The company's diversified product portfolio spans transmission equipment (AIS, GIS, transformers, reactors), generation (large gas and steam turbines), and clean energy solutions including PEM electrolyzers for green hydrogen and battery storage.
  • The company holds a robust order backlog of INR 150 billion entering FY25, translating to 2.1 times expected FY25 revenue, reflecting strong demand visibility.
  • The Indian T&D market size for base equipment has grown from INR 250 billion pre-COVID to INR 600-700 billion annually, fueled by renewable energy capacity expansion, grid modernization, and investment in HVDC technologies.
  • SEL commands leadership in the high-voltage segment, competing among global majors, with an addressable market of approximately INR 0.3 trillion for VSC-based HVDC technology.
  • Financially, SEL posted a 22.6% EBITDA margin in H1FY25, with FY27E projections of INR 105 billion revenues and INR 22.6 billion EBITDA representing ~21.5% margin.
  • The company is expected to deliver a 30% CAGR in PAT from FY25 to FY27, driven by new business lines related to hydrogen and battery solutions, robust order book conversion, and margins expansion.
  • Valuation is based on a 60x forward P/E multiple on Sep-27E EPS of INR 52.3, implying an INR 3,000 target price, around 27.7% upside from current levels, reflecting premium on growth, technology leadership and export opportunities.
  • Key risks include execution risks on new technology adoption, competition from large incumbents, and potential changes in Indian power sector policies or capital expenditure cycles.

Recommendation Rationale

  • Robust order backlog of INR 150 billion (~2.1x FY25 revenue) supports strong medium-term revenue growth expectations.
  • Strong leadership in technology and exclusive rights in South Asia markets provides competitive moat and growth runway.
  • Diversified portfolio across decarbonization, transmission, and generation segments positions the company well for India's energy transition.
  • Strong EBITDA margins (~22.6% in H1FY25) indicate operational strength and profitability.
  • 30% projected PAT CAGR over FY25-27 highlights compelling earnings growth.
  • Valuation at 60x Sep-27E EPS is justified given growth prospects and market positioning.
  • Limited competition in high-voltage segment due to technological barriers reduces earnings volatility and supports pricing power.
